In **amplitude modulation**, a *modulator* signal controls the amplitude of a
*carrier*. When the modulator is slow (a few hertz), we hear **tremolo**. When it
is in the audio range, new frequencies — **sidebands** — appear at the sum and
difference of the carrier and modulator frequencies.

**Ring modulation** is a close variant: AM with a bipolar modulator and no
constant offset, which suppresses the carrier and keeps only the sidebands.
