**Granular synthesis** builds sound from many short **grains** — typically 1 to
100 milliseconds each — overlapped in time. The composer controls the *cloud* of
grains rather than each sample: grain size, density, position, and pitch.

Granular techniques also underlie many time-stretching and texture tools.
