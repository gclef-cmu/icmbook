Sound is a **pressure wave**: a disturbance that propagates through a medium,
usually air. A microphone converts that changing pressure into a changing
voltage; an analog-to-digital converter turns the voltage into numbers.

## Attributes of a tone

A few physical quantities map, roughly, onto what we hear:

```{list-table}
:header-rows: 1

* - Physical quantity
  - Perceptual attribute
* - Frequency
  - Pitch
* - Amplitude
  - Loudness
* - Spectrum / waveform
  - Timbre
* - Duration
  - Length
```

The mapping is only approximate — perception is nonlinear and context-dependent —
but it is a useful starting point for synthesis.
